my research

Using optical flow to derive horizontal velocities of equatorial plasma bubbles

Abstract:

Computer vision has been used for a multitude of purposes, but the geosciences have yet to rigorously apply these algorithms to their fields. This paper attempts to use optical flow to calculate and track the velocities of equatorial plasma bubbles (EPB), plasma artifacts that is observed in the upper ionosphere. Previous work in the field has neglected to address the horizontal drift of these bubbles, so using optical flow might introduce new perspectives on the analysis of the data collected. Running the optical flow algorithm on actual data from the NOAA of EPBs, and the results confirm that the artifacts travel east. The significance of these bubbles is seen in scintillation, where data transmissions traveling through these bubbles diffract in unpredictable ways. Having better models will allow communications technology in explaining the reasons data is randomly lost in the atmosphere. Additionally, a better understanding of how the plasma bubbles move and the causes of this movement will lead to a better understanding of our atmosphere and those of other planets.
